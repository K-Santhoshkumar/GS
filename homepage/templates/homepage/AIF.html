What is an Alternative Investment Fund?

An AIF pools capital from eligible investors and invests per a defined strategy. Units are privately placed; subscription, valuation, and liquidity terms are governed by the fund documents and SEBI regulations.

  • Privately placed units, not offered to the public
  • Targeted strategies (venture, private credit, long-short, etc.)
  • Disclosure, custody, audit, and reporting as per regulations

Good to know

Eligibility, lock-ins, drawdowns, and liquidity can vary widely across AIF categories and strategies.

Category I

Invests in start-ups, early stage ventures, social ventures, SMEs, infrastructure, or other sectors deemed socially or economically desirable.

Category II

Funds that do not fall in Category I or III and do not undertake leverage other than for day-to-day operational requirements.

Category III

Employs diverse or complex trading strategies and may use leverage (e.g., long/short, derivatives) as permitted.
